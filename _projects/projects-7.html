Safety-Critical Multi-Agent MCTS for Unsignalized Intersections

Introduction

This research presents a safety-critical multi-agent Monte Carlo tree search (MCTS) framework for coordinating mixed traffic at unsignalized intersections. Our framework integrates deterministic and probabilistic predictions to enable cooperative decision-making in complex scenarios, with three key innovations: (1) a comprehensive safety assessment mechanism that systematically handles AV-to-AV, AV-to-HDV, and Vehicle-to-Road interactions; (2) an adaptive HDV behavior awareness approach combining the Intelligent Driver Model with probabilistic distributions; and (3) a multi-objective reward function that balances safety, efficiency, and cooperation.

Case 2: Mixed Traffic Scenario (AV Rate of Penetration = 50%)

To evaluate the robustness of our framework in mixed traffic scenarios, we conducted experiments with a 50% autonomous vehicle penetration rate. The test involves four AVs and four HDVs approaching the intersection from different directions, creating a more complex coordination problem due to the uncertainty in human driver behaviors.

Case 2.1: Four AVs and Four HDVs at Four-Way Intersection

In this mixed traffic scenario, HDVs with diverse driving styles (conservative, moderate, and aggressive) introduce additional complexity. The velocity profiles show larger fluctuations with wider confidence intervals, reflecting the increased uncertainty introduced by human drivers. Nevertheless, our framework successfully adapts to human driving behaviors while ensuring safe and efficient intersection crossing.

Conclusion

Our safety-critical multi-agent MCTS framework demonstrates superior performance in both fully autonomous and mixed traffic scenarios. The demonstrated balance between safety and efficiency, combined with the framework's ability to handle the inherent uncertainties of human drivers, suggests strong potential for real-world autonomous driving applications at unsignalized intersections.
